Recipe #1

Blueberry Muffins

  • Time: To 1,5h
  • Serves: 8 muffins
Ingredients Quantity
plain flour 110 g
butter 110 g
caster sugar 65 g
free-range eggs 2
baking powder 1½ tsp
blueberries 125 g
freshly grated nutmeg pinch

Step 1: Cream the butter and sugar together then slowly add the eggs, mix for three minutes. Add the flour, baking powder, nutmeg, stir to combine, then refrigerate for at least an hour, preferably overnight.

Step 2: Place a spoonful of muffin mixture into each muffin case, filling each to just over half way. Stud each muffin with about eight blueberries.

Step 3: Bake in an oven set at 200C/180C Fan/Gas 6 for 20 minutes, or until golden on top.

Recipe #2

Banana Bread

  • Time: To 1,5h
  • Serves: 10
Ingriedients Quantity
plain flour 285 g
butter 110 g
bicarbonate of soda 1 tsp
salt ½ tsp
caster sugar 225 g
free-range eggs 2
ripe bananas, mashed 4
vanilla extract 1 tsp
buttermilk 85 ml

Step 1: Preheat the oven to 180C.

Step 2: Sift the flour, bicarbonate of soda and salt into a large mixing bowl.

Step 3: In a separate bowl, cream the butter and sugar together until light and fluffy.

Step 4: Add the eggs, mashed bananas, buttermilk and vanilla extract to the butter and sugar mixture and mix well. Fold in the flour mixture.

Step 5: Grease a 20cm x 12.5cm/8in x 5in loaf tin (2lb) and pour the cake mixture into the tin.

Step 6: Transfer to the oven and bake for about an hour, or until well-risen and golden-brown.

Step 7: Remove from the oven and cool in the tin for a few minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely before serving.

Source: Banana Bread

Recipe #3

Macaroons

  • Time: To 3h
  • Serves: 10 macaroons
Ingriedients Quantity
ground almonds 125 g
icing sugar 200 g
free-range egg whites 3
caster sugar 2 tbsp
cream of tartar ½ tsp
red powdered food colouring pinch
dark chocolate, chopped 200 g
double cream 200 ml
brandy 1 tsp
unsalted butter 15 g

Step 1: Blend the ground almonds and icing sugar in a food processor until well combined. Set aside.

Step 2: Using an electric whisk, slowly whisk the egg whites in a large bowl at a low speed until stiff peaks form when the whisk is removed. Slowly whisk in the cream of tartar and caster sugar until the mixture is smooth and glossy, increasing the speed of the whisk as the mixture stiffens.

Step 3: Gently fold in the food colouring and blended ground almonds and icing sugar until the mixture resembles shaving foam.

Step 4: Spoon the macaroon mixture into a piping bag fitted with a 1cm/½in round nozzle. Pipe 5cm/2in circles onto the baking tray lined with greaseproof paper. If a peak forms, wet your finger and smooth it down. Sharply tap the bottom of the tray to release any air bubbles from the macaroons, then set aside for 60 minutes (the macaroon shells are ready to go in the oven when they are no longer sticky to the touch).

Step 5: Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 160C.

Step 6: Bake the macaroons in the oven for 10-15 minutes, or until cooked through. Remove from the oven and set aside to cool for 5 minutes. Carefully peel away the greaseproof paper and set aside to cool completely.

Step 7: Meanwhile, for the chocolate filling, heat the double cream and chocolate in a saucepan over a low heat, stirring occasionally, until smooth and well combined. Add the brandy and butter and stir until smooth, then remove from the heat and set aside to cool completely.

Step 8: Use the filling to sandwich the macaroons together then chill in the fridge for 30 minutes.

Source: Macaroons
